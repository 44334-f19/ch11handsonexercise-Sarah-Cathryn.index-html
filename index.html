  1. The Origins

    2. Copyright stems form the late fifteenth centry England when printers were required to register liscensed books. These where then able to be cencored by the British Government if the content was found controversial to the government. This was called the Licenseing Act of 1662, but was repealed by 1695. By 1710, Parliment established an act that would protect an authors works for up to fourteen years and were also renuable if the author was still alive by the experation date.For more information of the origins and in-depth time line Association of Research is a good place to start.

  4. What is Copywrite?

    5. Copywirte is the ownership of published and unpublished works ranging from literary works, to music to artwork. This isn't to be confused with a patent or material items. The basic copywrite laws we have today stem from the Constitution when authors wished for craters to have access and accesibility rights to their works. The three basic assesments to gain copywirte protections are originality, creativity, and fixation. Originality means that you were the person to create, write or compose a piece. Creativity means that there has to be a minimal amount of creativity that went into creating the piece. Fixation is the stability of the piece (i.e will it stand the weathers of time). For further information go to Copywrite Alliance.

    6. APurdue University gives some insight to what would happen it copywrite infringment where to occer.

To start prosecuting, the victum must have a valid copywrite license, the accused must have access to the sopywritted work, and the areas of accusation must be outside of the exscusable sections of a piece.
